Service Now

Duplicate Emails Error

As a part of my role as a software developer, I have the responsibility of resolving tickets raised by our users. When working on service now I open small tasks that can vary in their seriousness and importance. I usually deal with small tasks that users are facing and spend time fixing the bugs that may have arisen. This falls under the maintenance section of the software development life cycle and is a necessary part to all of our applications.

The example I have chosen is a user being unable to login to their account. It’s a simple problem but it can cause a large amount of stress for our users, especially around exam release dates. The initial ticket simply stated that a user was unable to login, even though there were no mistakes with their credentials.

I attempted to login on behalf of the user with our admin proxy ability and after some testing on my side of the application, I discovered that the user had duplicate accounts.

After confirming the duplicate email in our database, I updated the error ticket and notified my superiors. The system had saved two instances of the single user with two separate email addresses, and this caused the database to set them both as active, then the system locked as it can’t distinguish which account it should use. I made a simple SQL query to find all registered users with duplicate emails and grouped them together so I could find the one I was looking for.

The old pro and cold fusion systems allowed duplicate emails to be used, but the new system restricts access if it occurs. This was an unforeseen oversight from previous iterations and was an uncommon, but never the less disruptive issue. I notified the user about what happened and confirmed that multiple emails were entered when they were first registering. I deactivated the duplicate and made sure the other remaining account granted access to the site. I documented the error for future reference and I closed the ticket due to everything being resolved.

Exam Payment Error

When a user registers with us or attempts to pay for one of their exams, there is a very rare error that can potentially occur. It is where a user has received confirmation of their payment, my database will show the user has completed payment successfully but our finance team doesn’t receive all of the information about the user’s exam booking in their database. It may be due to a high volume of traffic, network issues or authentication difficulties.

A user enters their bank details and purchases an exam. The request goes to a payment system, (World Pay/ Western Union) which then checks if the bank details are correct. Once this is done, they accept the payment and send the authentication to our system. The “Request Log” then reads all of the information but if it fails for any reason or if the user is lost when being directed, it won’t update the users “Workflow”. This then leads to the finance team only having a sum of all the payments with a single Id and will lack all of the information such as “Exam Date”, “Exam Location”, “Product Code”, “Subject Description”, “Subject Code” and “Product Description”. All of these are crucial because without them, we are unable to generate a detailed invoice. Many users’ exams are funded by their companies or firms and the invoices are evidence that we acknowledge their payment.

Also, without all of the information, finance won’t be able to send the LPD (Learning Personal Development, responsible for booking and registering users for exams) team the exam request details, and the user will not be registered for their exam. This is devastating for the company’s reputation and is unacceptable, so a check is run by finance every single day to pick up on irregularities such as this one.

The check was run where an issue was found and I was asked to scan the database payments by running the specific query you see below.

This query will identify the missing payments in full detail as a JSON file, which I then used to extract the specific information needed for the finance team to book their exam.

Then I entered this into a Microsoft excel document manually, so that I could send it back to the finance department. They can then update their own systems, as it is clear the user has indeed paid for these exams but the confirmation failed.

The finance team then added the users details manually into their system and forwarded the update to the LPD team. This error is yet to be resolved with a permanent fix, so it is crucial that these checks are regularly performed.
